# FlightRefundClaim Tracking Link Playbook

## Why this matters

Every link in `tracked-campaign-links.csv` tells Analytics where the visitor came from. This means a future enquiry can be connected to the source that created it: Facebook, WhatsApp, Google Ads, community replies, email signature, or printed QR material.

Without these links, many visits appear as direct traffic, which makes it hard to know what is working.

## Simple rule

Use one tracked link per channel. Do not paste the plain homepage URL when promoting the business.

Examples:

- UK Facebook post: use the UK Facebook general post link.
- US traveller post: use the US Facebook general post link.
- WhatsApp message to someone in the UK: use the UK WhatsApp direct share link.
- Helpful reply in a group: use the matching community link, not the homepage.

## Naming pattern

- `utm_source`: the platform, for example `facebook`, `whatsapp`, `google`.
- `utm_medium`: the traffic type, for example `social`, `share`, `cpc`, `community`.
- `utm_campaign`: the broad campaign, for example `uk_organic_visibility`.
- `utm_content`: the exact placement, for example `general_post` or `profile_bio`.

Keep names lowercase with underscores. This keeps Analytics tidy.

## First weekly routine

1. Share two UK posts using the UK Facebook general post link.
2. Share two US posts using the US Facebook general post link.
3. Use the WhatsApp tracked link whenever you send the site manually.
4. Add the email signature link to outgoing business emails.
5. After one week, check Analytics for sessions and lead events by source.

## What counts as success

The first goal is not huge traffic. The first goal is learning which channel creates serious visitors.

Look for:

- visitors who start the claim checker,
- visitors who click WhatsApp,
- visitors who submit the contact form,
- visitors who submit a claim.

## Recommended first campaign names

- `uk_organic_visibility`
- `us_organic_visibility`
- `uk_community_answers`
- `us_community_answers`
- `uk_referral`

Flying home from Europe and your flight was delayed or cancelled? US passengers can still be protected by UK and EU flight compensation rules. Free eligibility check, no win no fee.

If your flight departed from the UK or EU and arrived more than 3 hours late, it may be worth checking eligibility. The rules depend on route, airline, delay length and the reason the airline gave.
